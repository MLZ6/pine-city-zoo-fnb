Learn more about our animals

Lion

The lion is known as the king of the jungle. These majestic big cats live in prides and are found mostly in Africa.
You will find the Lions at pen 22, next to the amphitheatre.

Elephant

Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth. They're known for their intelligence, memory, and strong family bonds.
You will find the Elephants at pen 21, next to the amphitheatre.

Giraffe

Giraffes are the tallest animals in the world. Their long necks help them reach leaves high up in trees.
You will find the Giraffes at pen 20, next to the amphitheatre.

Panda

Pandas are native to China and are known for their black and white fur. They mainly eat bamboo and are a symbol of conservation.
You will find the Pands at pen 19, next to the amphitheatre.

Gemsbok

The gemsbok is a large antelope native to arid regions of Southern Africa, known for its striking horns and resilience.
You will find the Gemsbok at pen 18, next to the amphitheatre.

Gorilla

Gorillas are the largest primates and live in family groups called troops. They are intelligent and share many traits with humans.
You will find the Gorillas at pen 17, next to the amphitheatre.

Koala

Koalas are marsupials native to Australia. They sleep up to 20 hours a day and feed mainly on eucalyptus leaves.
You will find the Koalas at pen 16, next to the amphitheatre.

Warthog

Warthogs are wild members of the pig family found in Africa. They're known for their tusks and ability to run quickly when threatened.
You will find the Warthogs at pen 15, next to the amphitheatre.

Monkey

Monkeys are clever and playful animals known for their agility and social behavior. They live in troops and often use tools.
You will find the Monkeys at pen 14, next to the amphitheatre.

Rhino Beetle

Rhino beetles are among the strongest creatures on Earth relative to their size. They're named for their horn-like structures.
You will find the Rhino-beetles at pen 13, next to the amphitheatre.
